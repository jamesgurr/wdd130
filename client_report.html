Junior Developer Report

How did you communicate with your team lead? Was there any issues?

I communicated with my team lead over on discord. I had a lot of questions and he was quick to address any questions and provide feedback for my work.

Expectations were clear. By viewing the site, the cascading sheet, and the site plan it was clear what needed to be done. Any clarifying questions were addressed and used as a plan for the specific web page I was tasked with.

The only issues I faced when publishing code to GitHub was remembering to pull after someone made a change. Otherwise GitHub was a straight forward platform.

General Experience and Reflections

What was the most challenging part of this project for you? Were you able to overcome it?

The most challenging part was getting started on deciding a site. Coding and styling wasn’t too much of an issue. The process to come up with an idea is both frustrating and tiring. The only way to overcome it was to try and think outside of the box. YouTube also helped when deciding on an idea.

What did you enjoy most about this project?

Collaboration was one of the best parts of this project. It was nice to get a perspective and an idea on what everyone else understood of the class. It’s clear that there are some students who are more gifted and talented at programming.

What would you do differently if you could do the project again?

The concept and site plan for mine was thrown together too quickly. If I were to go back I would definitely spend more time developing and working on a better idea, concept, and site plan.

Do you feel more confident in working on real-world projects after this experience? Explain.

I would say so. I feel though I would need more experience and maybe a good way to judge the process. Maybe by having others look at how the communication, the plan, and the execution turned
